# Product

## Register

product

## Users

ThreadTrace is primarily built for its creator-operator: a technical, curious, high-frequency forum researcher who wants a private intelligence cockpit for long-thread context, source monitoring, semantic review, and operational follow-through.

The user works in an exploratory mode: collecting source material, checking historical claims, restoring context around new posts, inspecting source health, and deciding what needs attention next. The product should feel fast, trustworthy, and personally empowering rather than like a corporate admin console.

## Product Purpose

ThreadTrace turns forum long threads and future source types into traceable context intelligence. It preserves raw evidence, author identity, floor/time metadata, relation hints, semantic interpretations, review tasks, source schedules, notification events, and operational ledgers so every conclusion can be traced back to evidence.

Success means the user can keep the system running daily, understand what changed, trust why a recommendation or warning appeared, and drill from a high-level signal down to the original post or operational record without losing context.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, technical, beautiful.

The product should feel like a young, sharp research cockpit: confident but not arrogant, technical but not hostile, polished but not decorative. It should make serious analysis feel easier to enter and more satisfying to operate.

## Anti-references

- Do not look like a generic admin template.
- Do not look like an ordinary enterprise system with flat white cards and default form controls.
- Do not look like a dark hacker terminal or cyberpunk dashboard.
- Do not collapse into a single-color theme.
- Do not feel like software made for old-fashioned enterprise users.
- Do not imitate loud AI SaaS landing pages, decorative gradients, or marketing-first product shells.

## Design Principles

1. Evidence earns trust.
   Every insight, alert, and review path should preserve a visible route back to source evidence, metadata, and audit records.

2. Personal cockpit over enterprise console.
   The interface should help one expert operator move quickly. It can be dense and powerful, but it must never feel like a bureaucratic back office.

3. Technical clarity with visual taste.
   The product should expose real system complexity through clean structure, restrained hierarchy, and tasteful color, not through noisy chrome or novelty effects.

4. Multi-source by default.
   NGA is the first source, not the architecture. Language, layout, and controls should leave room for future forums, feeds, connectors, and review destinations.

5. Young, sharp, and usable.
   The product may have personality and polish, but it must remain readable, accessible, and efficient during repeated daily use.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for normal text and controls. Preserve keyboard focus states, avoid meaning conveyed by color alone, keep motion restrained and reducible, and maintain responsive layouts that do not force horizontal scrolling on mobile or desktop.
